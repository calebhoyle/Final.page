Stage 4

2nd Print

After Redesigning and correcting those flaws I put the file into a splicer and printed the new and
improved design. Because my laptop could not run the splicing software and I couldn't wait for
the next week for Max to bring my next product into class, I found the 3d printers in the
lab downstairs that ran on the computers with the splicing software already downloaded. Once my new file
was loaded into the software, I put it into the printer using an SD card and began my print.
My first try was not successful. The print fell off-balance while in progress and the material webbed up creating
a mess that did not resemble my design. After this I added what is called a raft during the splicing
process. A raft is a base that the print can sit on and creates a balanced platform. After adding these improvements, the next print was successful and came out how I wanted it to.
